### How It Works

- **Load Balancing vs. True Bonding:**  
  Many multi-WAN routers can load balance two connections. This means traffic is distributed across both lines, which can increase overall throughput for multiple users or sessions. However, a single session (like a file download or video call) might not exceed the speed of one line unless you implement true bonding. True bonding aggregates both connections into one logical link, which typically requires both ends (your building and the remote server or VPN aggregator) to work together.

- **Equipment and Configuration:**  
  You’ll need a router or specialized bonding device (sometimes called a bonding router) that supports multiple WAN inputs. Some commercial devices and services (like Speedify or other bonding VPN solutions) can combine separate cable lines—even if they come from different providers.

- **ISP Involvement:**  
  In some cases, ISPs offer channel bonding directly on the modem level. If your cable lines are from the same provider, ask if they support bonding natively. If not, you’ll need a third-party solution to handle the bonding externally.

### Considerations

- **Cost and Complexity:**  
  Setting up a bonded connection might require additional hardware or subscription to a bonding service. The configuration can be more complex than a simple dual-WAN load balancing setup.

- **Application Requirements:**  
  Consider what you need from the bonding. For overall network redundancy and improved multi-user performance, load balancing might suffice. If you need increased speeds for single, bandwidth-intensive sessions, true bonding is necessary.

### Summary

Yes, you can bond two cable lines to your building. The right approach depends on your specific needs—whether it’s for redundancy, load distribution, or true aggregation of speeds—and on the capabilities of your equipment and ISP support.

If you’re planning to implement this, you might want to consult with a network specialist who can assess your setup and recommend the best bonding solution for your requirements.<br>
