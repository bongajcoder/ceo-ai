# Visitor Management Solutions for a Co-Working Startup

Choosing the right visitor management system is crucial for a co-working space. You’ll want a solution that offers **touchless check-in options** (NFC, QR codes, mobile apps) and manual sign-in with badge printing, plus **automation** (host notifications, scheduling, real-time occupancy tracking), **integrations** (calendars, CRM, payment systems), and support for **multiple visitor types** (members, event attendees, film crews, guests). Below is a comparison of several systems across a range of budgets – from free or affordable tools to high-end enterprise solutions – including features, pricing, scalability, pros/cons, and real-world examples.

## Key Features to Consider

- **Touchless Check-In** – Many modern systems support **QR code or mobile app check-ins** to allow visitors to sign in using their smartphone, avoiding shared touchscreens. Some systems also support **tablet kiosks** for self-service sign-in and can print name badges automatically. Envoy, for instance, uses an iPad-based app to capture visitor details, have them sign NDAs, take photos, and then **auto-print a badge via a connected printer**【47†L11-L18】. *(NFC-based check-in is less common out-of-the-box, but a few solutions integrate with access cards or NFC tags via connected hardware or access control integrations.)*

- **Automated Notifications & Workflows** – A good system will notify the host (member or staff) instantly when their guest arrives, via email, SMS, or chat apps. Most systems provide this by default (e.g. instant host alerts through Slack, Microsoft Teams, SMS, etc.【22†L209-L218】). Automation can also include **pre-registration** (sending invites or QR codes to visitors in advance), **scheduled visits**, and even rules like notifying staff if a visitor hasn’t checked out by a certain time. For instance, Teamgo (a budget-friendly option) supports **“overstay” alerts** if a visitor stays past their expected time【26†L144-L152】. Systems can often operate unattended, which is great for off-hours access – *e.g.* TechPlace (an innovation hub) set up 24/7 self-service check-in so that after-hours visitors could still sign in and be logged without staff present【42†L173-L181】.

- **Integration Capabilities** – Ensure the system can tie into your existing tools. Common integrations include **calendar apps** (Google Workspace, Outlook) to auto-create invites or pre-register visitors, **communication platforms** (Slack, Teams for host pings), **CRM or member management systems** (to turn visitor info into leads or contacts), and sometimes **access control systems** (to grant temporary door access). For example, Envoy can sync with Outlook/Google Calendar to email QR code invites and integrates with tools like Salesforce and Slack【26†L138-L147】【26†L143-L151】. Greetly is tailored for co-working and has direct integrations with co-working management platforms like essensys, Satellite Deskworks, OfficeRnD, and Yardi, eliminating manual data entry and syncing member/visitor data automatically【22†L191-L199】. - **Multiple Visitor Types & Custom Flows** – Co-working spaces host various guests: day-pass users, members’ clients, event attendees, filming crews, deliveries, etc. Look for software that lets you define **different sign-in workflows or check-in “buttons”** for each category. - **Capacity & Occupancy Management** – Given your space can host ~100 people normally and up to 200 during events, it’s important to track how many people are on-site in real time. Envoy, for example, allows you to **set capacity limits and will keep a live count of people on-site** to prevent overcrowding【25†L13-L21】. However, **automatically notifying guests who are “bumped” due to capacity** may require some integration – e.g. if an event registration system is tied in, it could email waitlisted attendees. In absence of a dedicated feature for bumping, an operator would need to monitor the capacity dashboard and use the system’s messaging or an email blast to inform extra visitors.

With **multi-location support**, if your startup grows to run multiple sites or a franchise model, Envoy scales effortlessly. Another pro is the continuous innovation – Envoy adds features (like their recent “Virtual Front Desk” to handle visitor intake via video call for unstaffed receptions)【16†L35-L43】, so you’re buying into an evolving platform. *Cons:* **Cost** is the biggest – for a bootstrapped startup space, Envoy’s paid plans might be prohibitive until you have significant revenue. While the free plan is great to test out, it lacks many core functions (no custom workflows, no capacity limits, no QR pre-reg on the free tier【26†L144-L152】【26†L148-L156】). Another consideration: Envoy is **iPad-exclusive for the kiosk**. If you prefer an Android tablet or a PC-based kiosk, that’s not an option. Also, with so many features, Envoy can be a bit **overkill for smaller operations** – some clients find they don’t use everything they pay for【4†L17-L26】【4†L31-L39】. If your startup only occasionally has visitors, a simpler solution could suffice. However, if you anticipate heavy traffic, complex requirements, and want a **tried-and-true system used by enterprise coworking spaces**, Envoy is a top candidate. Many upscale coworking brands have chosen Envoy for its reliability and ability to integrate into a broader smart workspace ecosystem (door access, Wi-Fi provisioning, etc.). It truly shines in environments where a high-end, seamless experience for guests is a priority.

**Proxyclick (Eptura)** – *Proxyclick* is another **enterprise-grade** visitor management system, comparable to Envoy in many ways. (It was acquired and is now part of Eptura, formerly Condeco, which is a workplace platform.) It focuses on security and compliance, serving clients like large corporates and multi-tenant buildings. **Features:** Proxyclick offers touchless QR sign-in (visitors get a QR code invite), customizable check-in flows, ID badge printing, watchlist screening, and **access control integration** (you can have it activate an access badge or turnstile for approved visitors). It also has a **companion smartphone app** for frequent visitors or employees to manage their invites. The system is highly configurable and supports multiple visitor types and multi-location management. **Integrations:** It integrates with Outlook/Google Calendar for invites, Slack and Teams for notifications, and a slew of enterprise systems (including Salesforce, Cisco Meraki for Wi-Fi provisioning, and access systems like Lenel or S2). **Pricing:** Proxyclick historically has a per-location subscription similar to Envoy. While exact prices aren’t public, an analysis by one third-party indicated an average cost of about **$12,000 per year** for Proxyclick in enterprise deployments【11†L7-L15】 (around $1000/month). They offer plans that likely start lower for a single location with basic features, but generally Proxyclick is aimed at mid-to-large organizations willing to invest. *Pros:* Very **robust and secure** – Proxyclick is known for its emphasis on data privacy and security (it’s GDPR compliant, etc.), which might appeal if your space handles sensitive clientele. It has an **“all features on all plans”** approach according to some sources【11†L37-L40】, meaning even the base subscription gives you the full feature set, and pricing mainly scales with number of entries or locations. This can be good if you want every integration (they don’t nickel-and-dime for add-ons). The **user interface** is professional and can be branded well. *Cons:* **Price** – Proxyclick can end up as costly as Envoy or more, especially if not on an annual contract. It’s likely overkill for a single small coworking site; its sweet spot is multi-site companies or buildings with high security needs. The company targets enterprises, so a startup coworking might find the sales process and support more geared to larger clients. If considering Proxyclick, it may be worth comparing it with Envoy to see which fits your exact needs and budget, as they are direct competitors. As a high-end solution, Proxyclick would give your space a cutting-edge experience similar to Envoy – but unless you have a specific feature of Proxyclick you need, many coworking startups stick with more cost-effective platforms until they scale up.

**Others in the High-End Category:** *Sine (Honeywell)* and *iLobby* are two other notable players. **Sine** offers a visitor system with a strong mobile app focus – visitors can use the Sine app to **check in based on geofencing or QR codes**, and Sine integrates deeply with access control and even **NFC ID tags**. Sine is used in corporate offices and construction sites; for coworking, it could be useful if you want the option for guests to check in via their phones without even scanning (e.g. the app detects they arrived on site). Honeywell (which acquired Sine) also emphasizes **compliance features** and occupancy monitoring, similar to Envoy. **iLobby** is a more security-focused system, often used in manufacturing or government facilities, but it also covers all visitor management basics and has options for **ID scanning and background checks**. These high-end solutions typically are priced on par with Envoy/Proxyclick (iLobby, for example, tends to be a custom quote, but their focus is more on secure facilities rather than flexible coworking scenarios).

## **Real-World Examples in Coworking**

To illustrate how these systems perform in environments like yours, here are a few brief case studies:

- **TechPlace (Ontario, Canada)** – TechPlace is a coworking and incubation space that welcomed over 5,000 visitors in its first year. They implemented *The Receptionist for iPad* and leveraged its customization to set up different sign-in buttons for various visitor types (entrepreneurs using hot desks, event attendees, meeting room guests, etc.). This allowed visitors to self-direct their check-in and provided TechPlace staff with rich data on who was using the space and why. *“The Receptionist makes our check-in process run virtually on its own,”* said their team – even guests coming in after hours could sign in and be logged, improving accuracy of their usage stats【42†L173-L181】. The result was a **hands-off, 24/7 reception** that impressed visitors (being very tech-forward) and saved staff time. TechPlace was able to easily pull reports on visitor demographics and purpose, which helped them demonstrate the hub’s impact to stakeholders【41†L25-L33】【41†L49-L58】.

- **Cultivated Synergy (Denver, USA)** – Cultivated Synergy is a niche coworking space serving the cannabis industry, where **legal compliance** for visitors is critical. They have waivers and legal forms every visitor must sign due to the nature of their members’ businesses. Initially, they used paper forms and found it overwhelming to manage as the number of daily visitors grew (especially during their frequent community events)【44†L25-L33】【44†L35-L40】. They turned to *The Receptionist for iPad*, and it transformed their process: all visitors now sign the required waivers digitally on the iPad, and those signed documents are **securely stored in the cloud**. This eliminated the need to physically store boxes of paper and made retrieving signed forms instantaneous, which is a huge benefit for compliance audits【45†L143-L151】【45†L148-L156】. CEO Ryan Tatum noted that he underestimated the need for a visitor system at first, but as the space grew, it became indispensable for both **efficiency and legal compliance**【45†L137-L145】【45†L153-L156】. This example shows that even for a space with very specific visitor requirements, a modern visitor management platform can adapt and simplify the workflow.

- **Enterprise Coworking Chains** – Large coworking operators have also publicly used visitor management tech. For instance, **Mindspace** (a global coworking provider) uses *Envoy* in many of its locations to streamline guest registration and security, and **WeWork** was reported to experiment with Envoy in some sites a few years back to handle the heavy flow of member guests and tour visitors. Envoy’s own site mentions that their robust plan at ~$329/mo is ideal for **coworking franchises and enterprise spaces** that demand advanced features across multiple sites【3†L215-L223】. While smaller coworking sites might start with cheaper tools, scaling up often leads them to Envoy or similar systems for the **enterprise-grade reliability and integration** (for example, tying into building access systems or corporate tenant directories in mixed-use buildings).

- **Member Management Integration** – Some coworking software suites incorporate visitor management. For example, OfficeRnD (a coworking management platform) can integrate with *Greetly* or *Envoy* to unify member and visitor data. One coworking space noted that using Greetly integrated with their member database meant **members could invite guests through the app, and those guests would automatically receive QR codes to check in**, updating the space’s occupancy count in real time. This kind of integration is highly valuable when juggling **multiple visitor types** – say, distinguishing between a **member’s guest** (who shouldn’t be charged a fee) and a **non-member walk-in** (who might need to purchase a day pass). With the right combo (e.g. Nexudus + Envoy【27†L59-L67】 or OfficeRnD + Greetly), the system can even prompt payment for a day pass or log a lead in CRM when an unaffiliated visitor signs in. These integrated case scenarios show that as your coworking space grows, you can connect your visitor system with billing or CRM systems to handle things like **bumping guests** when over capacity (e.g. automatically emailing them to reschedule) or offering them to **join a waitlist or pay for a reservation** in peak times – but this typically requires using the visitor management system’s API in combination with your booking system.

## **Summary: Choosing the Right Solution**

For a startup coworking space, the decision often comes down to balancing **budget vs. features**:

- If you need a **free or super-low-cost** solution to get started, **VisitUs’s free tier** or a lightweight app like **Vizitor ($35/mo)** can cover basic check-ins, QR code or tablet sign-ins, and notifications【33†L78-L87】【3†L261-L268】. These may lack deep integrations, but they present a professional step up from pen-and-paper without breaking the bank.

- In the **mid-range ($50–$150/mo)**, options like **SwipedOn** and **Greetly** provide more polished interfaces and valuable extras (like custom workflows, delivery management, and Slack/CRM integration). SwipedOn is a bit more budget-friendly (starting ~$49 and scaling up based on employees)【29†L185-L192】, while Greetly is tailored for coworking with unlimited usage at a flat rate【12†L319-L327】. If your space is focusing on growth and community-building, Greetly’s ability to turn visitors into leads and sync with coworking software can offer a strong ROI by boosting conversions【20†L51-L60】【22†L193-L201】.

- At the **high-end ($300+/mo)**, systems like **Envoy** and **Proxyclick** deliver enterprise-level reliability, security, and integration breadth. They are ideal if your space has reached a scale where managing hundreds of visitors a week with complex requirements (multiple locations, strict security protocols, etc.) is a reality. Envoy, for example, would let you enforce capacity limits automatically and impress VIP clients with a seamless, high-tech experience (pre-badged, escorted notifications, etc.)【25†L13-L21】【24†L239-L247】. The cost is significant, but so is the feature set – including insights and analytics that can help optimize how your space is used.

In deciding, consider **scalability**: a solution like The Receptionist or Greetly can grow with you (just adding locations or upping your plan as needed), whereas a free solution might need to be replaced after a point. Also, think about what matters most in your context: If you host frequent **events or film shoots**, the ability to pre-register guests with QR codes and handle large waves of check-ins (with maybe a “self check-in kiosk” plus an **overflow via personal phones**) will be key – many of the above systems can do that, but you’ll want one that doesn’t charge per check-in in that case. If **security and compliance** are crucial (for instance, ensuring every visitor signs a legal agreement and is accounted for), lean toward systems that emphasize document management and audit trails (The Receptionist, Envoy, etc. all store signed documents and logs securely【47†L27-L35】【45†L143-L151】).

Finally, take advantage of **free trials and demos**. Almost all these providers offer a trial period (14 days is common) or a live demo. Try one or two in your space to see how intuitive they are for your visitors and staff. Co-working is all about hospitality plus efficiency – the right visitor management system will impress guests with a smooth welcome (no long lines or confusion) while freeing your team from tedious tasks. With options ranging from free to premium, you can start with something simple and upgrade as your needs evolve, all while keeping your space secure, compliant, and user-friendly for the variety of visitors you’ll host.
